Who Issues FIRC? +
An FIRC (Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate) is an important document required to receive international payments in India. It serves as proof of the receipt of foreign currency and is often requested by regulatory bodies such as the RBI and the Income Tax Department for compliance and reporting purposes.

When an international payment is received, the inward remittance is facilitated by JP Morgan, USA. While the transaction passes through JP Morgan’s secure network, the actual funds are credited directly to the recipient’s Indian bank account. The FIRC is issued by the Indian bank where the amount is ultimately deposited.

This setup ensures a smooth and hassle-free process for businesses receiving international funds.
